Getting Started with BuildNest

Welcome to BuildNest! This documentation will help you make the most of our platform for building your tech portfolio through real-world projects.

Onboarding Process

After signing up, you will be guided through a simple 3-step onboarding process:

  • Skill Assessment: Tell us about your current skill level and interests
  • Profile Setup: Create your professional profile
  • Goal Setting: Define what you want to achieve on BuildNest

Platform Overview

BuildNest is designed to help you build a verified portfolio through micro-internships and peer collaboration. Key areas of the platform include:

  • Project Marketplace: Browse available projects
  • Workspace: Where you collaborate and complete projects
  • Portfolio Builder: Showcase your completed work
  • Community Hub: Connect with other people

Creating Your Profile

Your profile is your professional home on BuildNest. A complete profile helps you find better projects and collaborators.

Profile Components

A strong profile includes:

  • Professional Photo: Helps build trust with collaborators
  • Skills Matrix: Rate your proficiency in different technologies
  • Bio/Introduction: Share your background and goals
  • Learning Objectives: What you hope to achieve on the platform
  • Availability: How much time you can commit to projects

Pro Tip: Complete your profile to 100% to increase your visibility in search results and project applications by 40%.

Finding and Applying to Projects

The project marketplace is where you will find opportunities to apply your skills and build your portfolio.

Finding the Right Projects

Use our filtering system to find projects that match:

  • Skill Level: Beginner, Intermediate, or Advanced
  • Tech Fields: Filter opportunities based on your preferred role or area of focus within the industry.
  • Time Commitment: Projects ranging from 10 to 40 hours
  • Project Type: Solo, pair, or team projects

Applying to Projects

When applying for a project, you'll need to:

  • Write a compelling application message
  • Highlight relevant skills and experience
  • Propose your approach to the project
  • Estimate your timeline for completion

Working on Projects

Once you have joined a project, this section will guide you through the workflow for effective collaboration and project completion.

Project Workflow

  • Collaboration Tools: Use the platformâ€™s chat, file sharing, and task boards
  • Progress Updates: Regularly update your tasks and document progress
  • Review and Feedback: Share drafts for peer review before final submission
  • Final Delivery: Submit completed work along with documentation

Pro Tip: Consistently document your progress and maintain clear communication with your team to avoid confusion and missed deadlines.

Documenting Your Work

Documentation is at the heart of the BuildNest experience. It is what transforms projects into a compelling portfolio piece.

Reflection Framework

For each project, you will be guided to document:

  • The Problem: What challenge were you solving?
  • Your Approach: How did you plan to solve it?
  • Implementation: How did you execute your solution?
  • Challenges: What obstacles did you encounter?
  • Learnings: What did you learn from this project?
  • Future Improvements: What would you do differently next time?

Collaboration Best Practices

We promote transparent and team-friendly workflows:

  • Consistent documentation for code, design, and processes
  • Shared decision logs for accountability
  • Clear communication of tasks and responsibilities
  • Guides or runbooks to support onboarding and handovers

Giving and Receiving Peer Reviews

Peer reviews are essential for building credibility and improving your skills.

Review Guidelines

When reviewing others' work, focus on:

  • Constructive Feedback: Be specific and helpful
  • Code Quality: Evaluate readability and efficiency
  • Documentation: Assess clarity and completeness
  • Problem-Solving: Comment on the approach and creativity

Remember: Reviews should be professional and respectful. Personal attacks or non-constructive criticism violate our community guidelines.

Building Your Portfolio

Your portfolio is the culmination of all your work on the platform.

Portfolio Features

Your portfolio automatically includes:

  • All completed projects with your documentation
  • Verified peer reviews and ratings
  • Skill assessments based on completed work
  • Collaboration history on team projects
  • Customizable layout and presentation

Documentation Standards

We encourage clear and consistent documentation across all roles:

  • Comprehensive README or overview files with setup, usage, and purpose
  • Clear in-code or workflow comments for complex logic, designs, or processes
  • Architecture or design decision records for significant choices
  • API, data model, or integration documentation if applicable
  • User guides, test plans, and QA reports for quality assurance
  • Design mockups or style guides for UI/UX clarity
  • Deployment, monitoring, and operations runbooks for DevOps

Best Practices

Following best practices ensures your work is high quality, consistent, and professional.

General Guidelines

  • Consistency: Maintain a consistent structure, naming conventions, and formatting across all projects
  • Documentation: Document your code, decisions, and workflows clearly
  • Communication: Keep open and timely communication with collaborators and mentors
  • Version Control: Use Git effectively, commit often, and write meaningful commit messages
  • Testing: Verify your work with testing before submission or deployment
  • Learning: Continuously reflect and improve your skills after each project

Pro Tip: Applying best practices consistently helps you build a credible portfolio and improves collaboration with your peers.
